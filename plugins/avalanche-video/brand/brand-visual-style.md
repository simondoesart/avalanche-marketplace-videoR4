# Avalanche — Brand Visual Style (Video)

> v1 draft. This is the single source of truth for how our Higgsfield videos should look and move.
> Edit anything below — every generation reads from this file, so changing it here changes every future video.

---

## 1. Essence (the one-liner)

**Fast, precise, and warm-tech.** Motion that feels engineered, not chaotic. Clean geometry, deep space, a decisive red accent. Confident but never cold.

Three words to hold in mind on every shot: **Speed. Clarity. Depth.**

---

## 2. Color

| Role | Color | Hex | Use |
|------|-------|-----|-----|
| Primary accent | Avalanche Red | `#E84142` | The hero. Logo, energy, key light kicks, one focal object per frame. Never let a second color compete with it. |
| Base dark | Near-black | `#0A0A0B` | Default background / negative space. Deep, not muddy. |
| Base light | Off-white | `#F5F6F7` | Light-mode surfaces, clean product shots. |
| Cool support | Graphite / slate | `#2A2E33` | Mid-tones, planes, depth layers. |
| Signal (sparingly) | Cool white glow | `#FFFFFF` | Rim light, edges, spec highlights. |

**Rules**
- Red is a spotlight, not wallpaper. Roughly one red focal moment per shot.
- No rainbow gradients, no neon-purple "crypto" cliché, no gold coins.
- Backgrounds trend dark by default; go light only for clean product/UI shots.

---

## 3. Mood & Lighting

- **High contrast, controlled.** Deep blacks, crisp speculars, soft fill. Think product-film lighting, not music-video haze.
- **Single decisive key** with a subtle red kick or rim. Volumetric light OK if it stays disciplined.
- Avoid: lens-flare overload, heavy grain, dreamy pastel, VHS/retro-game looks (all common Higgsfield presets — skip them).

---

## 4. Motion Language

This is what makes it feel like *us*, not stock AI video.

- **Engineered camera moves:** smooth push-ins, precise orbits, locked-off reveals, parallax dollies. Motorized-rig feel.
- **Physics with intent:** elements assemble, snap, or resolve into order (fragments → structure). We move *toward* clarity, not away from it.
- **Pacing:** deliberate. One clear idea per shot. A beat of stillness at the end of a move ("settle").
- Avoid: handheld shake, whip-chaos, random drift, dance/character-meme energy.

Signature moves (map to building-block IDs): `slow-push`, `precise-orbit`, `assemble-in`, `parallax-reveal`, `earth-to-detail`.

---

## 5. Composition

- Generous negative space. Subject off-center or centered-with-air, never cramped.
- Strong horizon / grid logic. Geometry aligned, not tilted (no dutch angles unless intentional).
- Depth in layers: foreground element, hero subject, receding background.
- Aspect: `16:9` for hero/site, `9:16` for social, `1:1` for feed tiles.

---

## 6. Typography on frame (if text is burned in)

- Prefer clean geometric sans (Inter / Archivo / Montserrat family from Higgsfield subtitle fonts).
- Left-aligned or centered, tight tracking, generous margin. White or Avalanche Red only.
- Keep it minimal — the motion carries the message.

---

## 7. Do / Don't cheat-sheet

**Do:** deep black space · one red focal point · engineered camera moves · assembling geometry · clean product light · 4–8s decisive shots.

**Don't:** neon crypto clichés · gold coins / bull-bear imagery · pastel haze · retro-game / paparazzi / K-pop presets · handheld chaos · rainbow gradients · cluttered frames.
